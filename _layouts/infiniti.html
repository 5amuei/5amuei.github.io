Summary

Sam was a very personal young man, with a clear passion for mechanical engineering. I was impressed with his people skills and he scored well in the team events. I wish Sam good luck with his future career.

Andy Todd, Director, Body & Exterior Engineering, Nissan Technical Centre Europe.

Sam was a great sport with a fun attitude and isn’t afraid to speak up. He is able to think through issues and to focus on the task. I know that Sam will do well in the engineering world.

Greg Ramsey, Senior Manager, SV/NSA, INFINITI

Interview

Sam was good at building rapport with the judges. Some of his answers were not as strong as the other finalists. I would suggest Sam reflects on how he could have given more concise answers that still conveyed his technical knowledge and unique selling proposition.

Andy Todd, Director, Body & Exterior Engineering, Nissan Technical Centre Europe.

Sam seemed confident and at ease with the judges but at times came across as too laid back. When asked about what he was looking for in F1 and road cars, he appeared indecisive as if he hadn’t given it much thought. I recommend that in the future, Sam be prepared to provide a clear picture of where he wants to go and what he wants to do.

Greg Ramsey, Senior Manager, SV/NSA, INFINITI

Harvard Challenge

Sam did well in the Harvard challenge with a team that had some very vocal people with opposing views. Sam did a great job of keeping the team moving forward, clarifying points to make sure all were on the same page, and helped organize the team. Sam should consider taking on more of a leadership role in the future. He and the others on the team just assumed that Stan would present. Another lesson to be learned by everyone on the team: ask questions to make sure you have the data you need for the best decisions.

Greg Ramsey, Senior Manager, SV/NSA, INFINITI

Dragster Challenge


Sam had a strong contribution to make in the Dragster Challenge, despite the strength of Stan. The one aspect of his performance that really stood out was his strength of persuading and influencing his team mates. He made a strong impression, gained commitment from others, promoted his ideas well and negotiated well when conflict occurred. One area for Sam to consider was planning and organising – I only observed his group in the second half of the event, but I wondered if he had contributed ideas on how the team should organise the test runs during the free practise session.

Andy Todd, Director, Body & Exterior Engineering, Nissan Technical Centre Europe.

I observed the second half of the challenge so didn’t get to see how the team started up. Sam made a good impression as I observed him being a key part of the team. Ultimately, the team did not perform well in the races. Although there was discussion on information and recommendations, there seemed to be 3 separate groups doing their own thing. I encourage Sam to look for opportunities to influence and bring everyone together as a more cohesive team since he has the communication skills and approachability for it.

Greg Ramsey, Senior Manager, SV/NSA, INFINITI
